14.1
Quadratic equation
A degree-2 polynomial equation — the highest exponent is 2. Looks like ax² + bx + c = 0.
Example
x² + 5x + 6 = 0
Degree 2 → quadratic
14.1
Factor (noun)
A number or expression multiplied with another to get a product. 3 and 4 are factors of 12.
Example
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of x² + 5x + 6: (x+2)(x+3)
14.1
Factoring
Breaking down a polynomial into its factors — distribution in reverse / multiplication backwards.
Example
6x² + 9x
= 3x(2x + 3)
(factored form)
14.1
Factoring is actually just…
Distribution in reverse / multiplication backwards. If you can distribute, you can factor — just think about what was multiplied together to get the expression.
14.1
Greatest Common Factor (GCF)
The largest factor appearing in ALL terms of the expression. Always factor this out first, before any other technique.
Example
12x³ − 8x² + 4x
GCF = 4x
= 4x(3x² − 2x + 1)
New in 14.2
14.2
Very first step when factoring is…
Always factor out the GCF first — even if the GCF is 1. This is the rule before any other technique.
14.2
You can use the shortcut when…
a = 1 (the leading coefficient is 1). For x² + bx + c, find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b.
14.2
Split the Middle Term
A technique for factoring when a ≠ 1: multiply a·c, find two numbers for that product and the middle sum, then rewrite the middle term as two terms and factor by grouping.
Example
2x² + 7x + 3
a·c = 6; find 1 and 6
2x²+x+6x+3
= (x+3)(2x+1)
14.2
X Method
A visual tool: write the product on top of the X and the sum on the bottom. Find the two numbers that fill in the sides.
Example
x² + 7x + 12
Top = 12, Bottom = 7
Sides = 3 and 4
New in 14.3
14.3
When the first term is negative…
Factor out −1 first! Change all signs inside, then proceed with normal factoring techniques. Example: −x² + 5x − 6 = −1(x² − 5x + 6) = −(x−2)(x−3).
14.3
Difference of Squares
a² − b² = (a + b)(a − b). Two perfect squares separated by subtraction. Always factors into conjugate binomials.
Example
x² − 25 = (x+5)(x−5)
4x² − 9 = (2x+3)(2x−3)
Sum of squares (a²+b²) does NOT factor!
New in 14.4
14.4
Zero Product Property (ZPP)
If A · B = 0, then A = 0 OR B = 0. The only way a product is zero is if at least one factor is zero.
Example
(x+2)(x+3) = 0
x+2 = 0 → x = −2
x+3 = 0 → x = −3
14.4
How to solve equations by factoring
Move everything to one side = 0, factor completely, apply Zero Product Property (set each factor = 0), solve each linear equation, check both solutions.
